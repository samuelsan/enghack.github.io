Tech Retreat

August 15-16, 2015 | University of Waterloo

Learn. Collaborate. Create.

The Waterloo Tech Retreat brings together passionate high school students at a two-day event held in the heart of Canada's technology hub. Hear from seasoned speakers and students in the industry. Learn and collaborate with like-minded peers to create something amazing.

Venue

Engineering 5, University of Waterloo

No experience? No problem. We've got mentors here to help you out, every step of the way!

Questions? Get in touch!

Hackathon

Join us from 8:30 AM on August 15 to 7:00 PM on August 16 to create something cool with a team of up to 4 people.

A hackathon is an event where participants can work on any software and hardware project of their choosing without distraction. At the end of the competition you will be given the opportunity to present your project to the audience for a chance at winning one of the various prizes. There will be mentors and volunteers to assist you at every step of the way.

Learnathon

Join us on 8:30 AM on August 15 to 7:00 PM on August 16 to learn about technology with a team of up to 4 people.

Participate in workshops with like-minded peers to learn about software and hardware programming in a fun casual atmosphere. Get the opportunity to talk to leaders from the industry, and upper-year university students completing math and engineering degrees!

And don't worry about food and drinks - It's on us.

FAQs

Q: Can I come?

A: If you are a High school student, or if you just recently graduated from high school and would like to attend before university, then yes!

Q: I have no/little experience with technology - can I still come?

A: Tech Retreat focuses on having fun, learning, and collaborating. We're actually designing it so that students with no experience will have an awesome time! If you get stuck on something, don't worry about it - there will be mentors that can help.

Q: As a high school student, why do I want to come to this retreat?

A: You’ll also be spending time with awesome people who you’ll get to meet and form friendships with. In addition to everything above, there will also be a student panel to talk about their university experiences. We'll also be providing a free stay overnight at a Waterloo residence, so you'll get to see what it's like to live in res.

Q: How much is this going to cost?

A: It's going to be absolutely free! We'll also provide food for both days!

Q: What should I bring to the event?

A: A laptop, a charger, a water bottle, toiletries, bedding, and your brilliant mind.
